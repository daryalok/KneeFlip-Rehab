# KneeFlip Rehab

KneeFlip Rehab is a small Flipper Zero app for following knee rehabilitation routines prescribed by a clinician.

It is meant to be a simple offline companion during recovery: start an ice or elevation timer, pace through exercises, write down pain and swelling notes, record range-of-motion progress, and export a basic log when you want to share it later.

> **Disclaimer:** KneeFlip Rehab does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. It is only a timer and journaling tool. Always follow the recovery plan given to you by your surgeon, doctor, or physical therapist.

## What it does

- Ice and elevation timers
- Exercise pacing with vibration and sound cues
- Quad sets timer
- Heel slides counter
- Pain before and after a session
- Swelling notes
- Manual range-of-motion entries
- CSV export to microSD
- Simple recovery history

## Why Flipper Zero?

Rehab is repetitive, and the useful tool is often the one that is already nearby. Flipper Zero has a small screen, physical buttons, vibration, sound, and microSD storage, so it can work as a pocket-sized recovery log without needing a phone, an account, or an internet connection.

## Example use cases

- Tracking post-op knee recovery sessions
- Following clinician-prescribed exercise timing
- Logging pain and swelling changes over time
- Recording knee flexion progress
- Exporting recovery notes for a doctor or physical therapist

## Data export

Logs are intended to be written as simple CSV files on the Flipper Zero microSD card. The goal is to keep the data plain and easy to inspect, copy, or share with a clinician.

## Status

This project is early and intentionally focused. It is not trying to replace a medical app or a physical therapy program; it is a small helper for staying consistent with a recovery routine you already have.
